Description

Jajangmyeon, a beloved Korean-Chinese noodle dish, is a tantalizing combination of thick wheat noodles bathed in a rich and savory black bean sauce. With its origins tracing back to the early 20th century in China, this dish has become a staple in Korean cuisine, adored by locals and international food enthusiasts alike.

At its heart, jajangmyeon is defined by the distinctive black bean sauce that gives it its dark, luscious appearance. The sauce is made by stir-frying fermented black bean paste, known as chunjang, along with a medley of ingredients such as diced pork, onions, and various vegetables. This careful blend of flavors creates a complex and deeply satisfying taste profile, characterized by its umami-rich and slightly sweet notes.

Served piping hot, the cooked noodles are generously coated in the velvety black bean sauce, creating a delightful marriage of textures and tastes. The chewy and springy noodles perfectly complement the robust flavors of the sauce, resulting in a harmonious blend that satisfies the palate. Jajangmyeon is often garnished with thinly sliced cucumbers and enjoyed alongside tangsuyuk (sweet and sour pork) or pickled radish, adding a refreshing contrast to the savory main dish.

Whether enjoyed in the comfort of a local Korean restaurant or prepared at home, jajangmyeon offers a truly unique and indulgent dining experience. Its cultural significance and widespread popularity have made it a beloved dish, cherished for its distinctive flavors and comforting nature. A bowl of jajangmyeon is not just a meal; it is a journey into the rich culinary heritage and vibrant flavors of Korea.

Ingredients

  • 200 grams thick wheat noodles
  • 150 grams pork belly or pork loin, diced
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 3 tablespoons chunjang (fermented black bean paste)
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 1 tablespoon oyster sauce
  • 2 tablespoons potato starch or cornstarch
  • 1 cup water
  • 1/2 cup diced zucchini
  • 1/2 cup diced potatoes
  • 1/2 cup diced carrots
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • Thinly sliced cucumbers, for garnish

Instructions

  1. Cook the thick wheat noodles according to the package instructions. Once cooked, drain and set aside.
  2. In a large pan or wok, heat the vegetable oil over medium heat.
  3. Add the diced pork and saut√© until it starts to brown.
  4. Add the chopped onion and minced garlic to the pan, and continue cooking until the onion becomes translucent.
  5. Add the diced zucchini, potatoes, and carrots to the pan, and stir-fry for a few minutes until they start to soften.
  6. In a small bowl, dissolve the potato starch or cornstarch in the water, and set aside.
  7. Move the ingredients to one side of the pan and add the chunjang (fermented black bean paste) to the empty side of the pan. Stir-fry the chunjang for a minute to release its flavors.
  8. Combine all the ingredients in the pan, and stir well to evenly distribute the black bean paste.
  9. Add the sugar and oyster sauce to the pan, and continue stirring to coat the ingredients.
  10. Pour in the dissolved potato starch or cornstarch mixture, and stir constantly until the sauce thickens.
  11. Reduce the heat and let the sauce simmer for a few minutes, allowing the flavors to meld together.
  12. Divide the cooked noodles into serving bowls, and spoon the jajang sauce generously over the noodles.
  13. Garnish with thinly sliced cucumbers.
  14. Serve hot and enjoy your delicious homemade Jajangmyeon!
