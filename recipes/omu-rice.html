Description

Omurice is a beloved Japanese dish that skillfully combines the flavors of fried rice and a luxurious demi-glace sauce. This culinary delight features a fluffy omelet enveloping a savory rice filling, enhanced by the rich umami notes of the demi-glace sauce. Originating in Japan, omurice has captivated taste buds worldwide with its comforting and delectable appeal.

To prepare omurice, fragrant fried rice is crafted with diced onions, carrots, and a choice of protein such as chicken or beef. Seasoned with a hint of soy sauce and other spices, the rice becomes a flavorful foundation for the dish. Carefully enveloped within a delicate omelet, the rice showcases a delightful contrast between the creamy exterior and the succulent rice inside. The pièce de résistance is the demi-glace sauce—a velvety reduction sauce crafted from rich stock, aromatic vegetables, and herbs. This heavenly sauce is generously poured over the omurice, infusing each mouthful with a luxurious depth of flavor.

Omurice presents a harmonious blend of textures and tastes that tantalize the senses. The fluffy omelet, tender rice, and umami-packed demi-glace sauce combine to create a symphony of flavors that both comfort and inspire. Whether relished as a beloved home-cooked meal or savored in authentic Japanese establishments, omurice showcases the culinary ingenuity that arises from the fusion of diverse cuisines.

Ingredients

Fried Rice

  • Chicken (chopped)
  • Jasmine rice
  • Butter
  • Onion (diced)
  • Garlic cloves (minced)
  • Mixed vegetables (carrots, peas, corn, etc.)
  • Soy sauce
  • Salt
  • Black pepper
  • Optional: Chili flakes or hot sauce for added spice
  • Optional: Fresh herbs (such as cilantro or parsley) for garnish

Omelette

  • Eggs
  • Half & Half
  • Olive Oil

Demi-glace

  • Butter
  • Onion (finely chopped)
  • All-purpose flour
  • Tomato paste
  • Consommé (beef or chicken)
  • Tonkatsu sauce
  • Ketchup
  • Soy sauce
  • Sugar
  • Salt
  • Black pepper
  • Optional: Carrot (finely chopped)
  • Optional: Celery (finely chopped)

Directions

Fried Rice

  1. Cook the jasmine rice according to the package instructions. Once cooked, set it aside to cool slightly.
  2. Heat a large pan or wok over medium heat and add butter. Allow it to melt and coat the bottom of the pan.
  3. Add the diced onion to the pan and sauté until translucent and slightly caramelized.
  4. Add the minced garlic to the pan and sauté for an additional minute until fragrant.
  5. If using, add the finely chopped carrot and celery to the pan and stir-fry for a few minutes until they start to soften.
  6. Push the vegetables to one side of the pan and add the chopped chicken to the other side. Cook until the chicken is fully cooked and lightly browned.
  7. Combine the chicken and vegetables together in the pan and push them to one side.
  8. Add the cooled jasmine rice to the other side of the pan. Break up any clumps and stir-fry the rice for a few minutes, allowing it to heat through.
  9. Drizzle soy sauce evenly over the rice, and season with salt and black pepper to taste. Mix well to ensure the flavors are distributed evenly.
  10. If desired, add a sprinkle of chili flakes or hot sauce for some added spice.
  11. Optional: Add any additional mixed vegetables (such as peas or corn) to the pan and stir-fry for another minute or until they are heated through.
  12. Remove the pan from the heat and garnish with fresh herbs, such as cilantro or parsley, if desired.

Omelette

  1. Whisk eggs with milk, salt, and pepper.
  2. Heat a non-stick pan and add butter or oil.
  3. Pour half of the egg mixture into the pan and cook until golden.
  4. Fold one-third of the omelette towards the center.
  5. Add more butter or oil to the pan and pour the remaining egg mixture.
  6. Cook until the bottom is golden and the top is almost set.
  7. Fold the remaining third of the omelette towards the center.
  8. Transfer the omelette to a plate, folded side down.
  9. Optional: Shape the omelette using plastic wrap or a towel.
  10. Remove the wrap or towel if used.
  11. Serve the omelette on top of the fried rice.
  12. Optionally, garnish with tonkatsu sauce or ketchup.
  13. Slice the omelette before serving.

Demi-glace

  1. In a saucepan, melt butter over medium heat.
  2. Add finely chopped onions to the pan and sauté until they become translucent and slightly caramelized.
  3. Sprinkle in all-purpose flour and stir continuously for a minute to create a roux.
  4. Add tomato paste to the pan and continue stirring for another minute to cook off the raw flavor.
  5. Slowly pour in consommé (beef or chicken) while stirring constantly to incorporate the liquid into the mixture.
  6. Add tonkatsu sauce, ketchup, and soy sauce to the pan, stirring well to combine.
  7. Season the sauce with sugar, salt, and black pepper to taste.
  8. Reduce the heat to low and let the sauce simmer for about 15-20 minutes, stirring occasionally to prevent sticking.
  9. If the sauce becomes too thick, you can add a little more consommé or water to achieve the desired consistency.
  10. Once the sauce has thickened and the flavors have melded together, remove it from the heat.
  11. Optional: For a smoother texture, strain the sauce through a fine mesh sieve to remove any lumps or bits of onion.
  12. The Demi-glace sauce is now ready to be used as a flavorful topping for your omurice.
  13. Spoon the sauce generously over the fried rice and omelette, or serve it on the side.
  14. Enjoy your omurice with the delicious Demi-glace sauce!

Plating

  1. Place a portion of the fried rice in the center of a serving plate.
  2. Using a spatula, shape the rice into a neat mound or make a well in the center for the omelette.
  3. Carefully slide the cooked omelette onto a cutting board or a clean surface.
  4. With a sharp knife, make a lengthwise cut down the center of the omelette, stopping just before reaching the end.
  5. Gently open up the slit to reveal the tender and slightly runny interior of the omelette.
  6. Transfer the opened omelette and place it on top of the fried rice, covering it partially or entirely.
  7. If desired, drizzle a generous amount of the Japanese Demi-glace sauce over the omelette and rice, allowing it to cascade down the sides.
  8. Garnish the dish with a sprinkle of chopped parsley or green onions for a pop of color and freshness.
  9. Optionally, add a dollop of ketchup or tonkatsu sauce on top of the omelette for additional flavor.
  10. Serve the Omu-rice immediately while still hot and visually appealing.
  11. Encourage your guests to break into the omelette, allowing the luscious egg to meld with the flavorful fried rice.
  12. Enjoy the delightful combination of textures and flavors in every bite.
